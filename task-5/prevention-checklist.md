# Stay Safe Online: A Prevention Checklist

*Actionable steps for college students in India*

1. **Secure Your UPI Transactions**: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Scammers often use "Request Money" links to trick users into authorizing a deduction from their own account.

2. **Verify UPI IDs Before Sending**: Always double-check the name of the recipient in the UPI app (e.g., BHIM, Google Pay, PhonePe) before hitting `Pay` to ensure it matches the intended person or merchant.

3. **Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**: Enable 2FA on your university portals, email, and social media accounts. Use an authenticator app rather than just SMS to prevent SIM-swapping attacks.

4. **Audit App Permissions**: Regularly check which apps have access to your contacts, microphone, and gallery. Many "instant loan" apps or shady gaming apps harvest this data for blackmail.

5. **Be Skeptical of 'Job' or 'Scholarship' Links**: Do not click on unsolicited WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering high-paying part-time jobs or government scholarships that require a "registration fee."

6. **Secure Public Wi-Fi**: Avoid logging into bank accounts or student portals while on campus or cafe Wi-Fi. If necessary, use a trusted VPN to encrypt your connection.

7. **Check for HTTPS**: Ensure any website where you enter credentials or personal data has a padlock icon and starts with `https://`.

8. **Regular Software Updates**: Keep your smartphone's OS and apps updated to the latest version to patch security vulnerabilities that hackers exploit.
